Homesick: Loving Home Without Living There

Missing home isn’t weakness. It’s grief — and grief is love with nowhere to go.

Homesickness gets brushed off as something only children feel. It isn’t. If you ache for home — the people, the places, the ease of belonging — that ache is real, and it deserves better than “but you wanted this.” This month we’ll treat it as what it actually is: grief. And we’ll find ways to stay close to home that leave you fuller, not emptier.

What’s actually happening

You didn’t just change addresses. You lost a whole world.

When you moved, you left more than a place. You left the people who knew your history without explanation. The streets that held your memories. The ease of belonging without trying. The version of you that made sense there — whose jokes landed, whose competence was obvious. That’s a genuine loss, and the mind grieves losses, even chosen ones.

Grief isn’t weakness, and it isn’t ingratitude. You can be truly glad you came and ache for what you left. Both are allowed to be true.

Here’s where it gets sneaky. We try to soothe the missing by staying glued to home online — the group chats, the stories, the feeds. But watching your old life carry on without you usually deepens the wound rather than closing it. And everyone posts their highlight reel, so you end up comparing your hard middle to everyone else’s best moments.

There’s a difference between connection that fills you and contact that drains you. A real call with someone who asks how you actually are fills you. Scrolling your old friends’ weekend leaves you emptier.

The goal this month isn’t to cut off home — it’s to connect with it in the ways that leave you warmer, and to gently limit the ways that leave you hollow.

The exercise

Connection that fills vs. contact that drains.

Not all “staying in touch” is equal. Let’s sort yours honestly — then design one act of connection you know leaves you fuller.

Which ways of connecting to home leave you feeling fuller — warmer, closer, more yourself?
Which leave you feeling emptier — sadder, more behind, more left out?
Design one act of connection this week that you know fills you — and make it specific (who, when, how).
The grounding practice

Home in My Body

We won’t push the missing away. We’ll let home be with you for a moment — and remember you carry it inside, not only behind you.

1
Settle — Eyes closed if that’s okay. One slow breath out.
2
Picture home — A room, a street, a face that has meant home. Let the details come: the light, a smell, a sound.
3
Let it ache — If it aches, let it. That ache is just love with nowhere to go right now. It’s allowed.
4
Bring it in — Hand on your heart. Let the warmth of that place settle there. Home isn’t only what you left — it’s something you carry.
5
Hold both — Open your eyes to the room you’re in now. You can hold both: where you came from, and where you are. Both are yours.
