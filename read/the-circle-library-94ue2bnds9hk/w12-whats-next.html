What’s Next

Wherever you go from here, you go as someone this chapter made wider.

Every chapter abroad eventually asks a question: stay, go home, or move on again? It can feel heavy — and there’s a twist no one warns you about: “home” may not feel like home anymore, because you’ve changed and it didn’t. This month is about facing that question on purpose, and gathering up everything this chapter gave you — whatever you decide.

What’s actually happening

You can’t quite go home — because you’re not quite the same.

Here’s the part that catches people off guard: going back is often harder than going out. You expect ease and familiarity — but you’ve grown in ways the old place can’t see, life there carried on without you, and almost no one fully gets what you’ve been through. That’s reverse culture shock, and the quiet grief of it (“you can’t go home again”) is real.

But notice what that grief is actually evidence of: you changed. Whatever you decide next, you are not the person who got on that plane. You’ve done a genuinely hard thing — you’re more adaptable, more aware, more able to be uncomfortable and keep going. That doesn’t wash off at the border.

Whatever you choose from here, you choose it as someone steadier, braver, and wider than the person who first arrived. That doesn’t leave you.

One gentle caution: a lot of people drift into the next move — by visa, by job, by inertia — rather than choosing it. You’ve earned the right to decide on purpose. So the real question isn’t just “what’s easiest?” It’s “what do I actually want the next chapter to be?”

The exercise

Carry it forward — on purpose.

Before you decide anything, let’s gather what this chapter gave you — and get honest about what you want next.

What has this chapter abroad given you that you’ll keep, wherever you go next?
Who have you become that the person who first arrived couldn’t have imagined?
If you’re choosing the next step: what do you want the next chapter to feel like — not what’s easiest or default?
The grounding practice

Who I’ve Become

However you got here, let’s take a moment to see how far you’ve come.

1
Breathe — One slow breath, all the way out.
2
Meet your earlier self — Picture the version of you who first arrived — overwhelmed, disoriented, googling how to do simple things. Look at them kindly.
3
Look at now — You’ve learned a world from scratch. Been lonely and kept going. Built anchors, found a few people, made a life where there was none. That earlier you couldn’t have imagined this you.
4
Name the keepsake — One thing this chapter gave you that you’ll carry, wherever you go next.
5
Carry it — “Whatever I choose next, I choose it as who I’ve become — and that’s mine now.” Breathe it in. You did this.
