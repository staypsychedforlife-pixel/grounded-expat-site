Running Toward, or Running Away?

An honest, gentle look at what’s really driving your life on the move.
Created by Stephanie Johnson, LICSW

Every mobile life is driven by two engines at once: moving toward something you want, and moving away from something you would rather not face. Most of us are doing both, in a mix we have never quite looked at directly. This journal is that look. Not to judge the motion, and not to talk you out of a life you love, but to make sure you are the one choosing it, with your eyes open.

Across two parts it separates the freedom from the flight, gently, and helps you turn constant motion into a conscious choice rather than a quiet compulsion. Part One

The Two Engines

What’s actually driving the motion

Toward and Away

Underneath a life in motion are two forces that usually travel together: the pull toward a life you want, and the push away from one you did not. Naming both, without deciding one is good and one is bad, is where honesty begins.

A note for you

There is no shame in either engine. Wanting more and avoiding pain are both deeply human. The goal is not to have only pure motives; it is to see clearly what is moving you, so you stay in the driver’s seat.

SFrom my work

Most people arrive believing their motion is all ‘toward,’ all adventure and growth, or, in a harder moment, they fear it is all ‘away,’ all escape. It is almost always both, in a ratio that shifts over time. The people I have seen stay genuinely free are not the ones with the purest motives. They are the ones honest enough to notice when the ‘away’ has quietly taken the wheel, and brave enough to say so.

— Stephanie, LICSW

When you think about why you keep moving, what are you moving toward?
And, honestly, what might you be moving away from?
Which engine feels stronger right now, the toward or the away? Has that shifted over time?
What have you never quite let yourself admit about why you live this way? one small thing

What Stillness Brings Up

The clearest test of what is driving you is not motion; it is stillness. Notice what rises the moment you imagine staying somewhere for a long time, with no next place booked. Relief and dread both tell you something true.

A note for you

Whatever comes up when you imagine standing still is information, not a verdict. Dread does not mean you have to stop; relief does not mean you have to settle. It just shows you what the motion has been holding at bay.

Picture staying in one place for two years, nothing booked, no exit planned. What is the very first feeling that rises?
When you imagine staying still for a long time, is it more relief, or more dread?
Then some part of you is tired of moving, and stillness sounds like rest. That is worth listening to.
What are you tired of that stillness would relieve?
What has the constant motion been costing you that you are ready to stop paying?
Then stillness brings something up, and the motion may be keeping it at bay. Let’s meet it gently.
What do you imagine you would have to feel, or face, if you stopped moving?
Is that thing actually dangerous, or just deeply uncomfortable to sit with?

What You Might Be Outrunning

If part of the motion is flight, it helps to know from what. Not to shame it, but because a thing you can name loses some of its grip. It might be a feeling, a grief, a relationship, a version of yourself, or simply the fear of standing still long enough to be truly known.

A note for you

Whatever you have been outrunning, you are allowed to turn and look at it from a safe distance, on paper, in your own time. Naming it is not the same as being caught by it. It is how you stop being chased.

If some of your motion is running from something, what is the something? Let yourself guess, even if you are not sure.
When did the running start? What was happening in your life then?
What are you afraid would happen if you stopped and let it catch up?
What would it feel like to face even a small piece of it, from where you are now, safe? one small thing

Leaving Before You’re Left

For some, the motion is not only about escaping a place; it is about controlling a goodbye. If you leave first, you cannot be left. It can feel like freedom, but underneath it is often an old fear of being the one who stays and gets hurt. This is a tender one, so go gently.

A note for you

If you learned to leave before you can be left, you learned it for a reason, probably a good one, once. It kept you safe. Naming it does not mean you are broken; it means you get to choose differently now, from safety instead of fear.

Do you tend to leave relationships or places before they can leave you? Where do you notice that pattern?
When you sit with it, does ‘leaving before I’m left’ feel true for you?
Then this is an old form of self-protection, and it deserves compassion, not shame.
When did you first learn that leaving first felt safer than being left?
What might become possible if you let yourself stay a little longer than the fear wants you to? one small thing
Then that particular pattern may not be yours, and that is good to know too.
What does drive your timing, then, when you decide it is time to move on?
Is that timing coming from you, or from something you would rather not sit with?
Part Two

Choosing on Purpose

Motion as a choice, not a compulsion

The Freedom vs. The Flight

Freedom and flight can look identical from the outside: the same flights, the same new cities. The difference is on the inside. Freedom moves toward; flight moves away. Freedom expands; flight escapes. Learning to feel that difference in your own life is the heart of this work.

A note for you

You do not have to give up your freedom to give up your flight. The same life can be lived from either place. The invitation is simply to move from wanting, more often than from fear.

In your life, when does moving feel like freedom, expansive, chosen, alive?
And when does it feel more like flight, driven, restless, a way to not-feel?
How can you tell the difference in your own body, in the days before you decide to move on?
What would help you catch it in the moment, when flight is dressing up as freedom? exercise

If You Weren’t Running

Here is a useful thought experiment: imagine the running were simply not an option for a while. Not forever, just paused. What would you have to build, face, or feel? What would you actually want, if leaving were not on the table?

A note for you

The point of this is not to trap you anywhere. It is to reveal what you would choose if choice, not escape, were the only thing driving you. That is the life worth building, whether you stay in motion or not.

If you could not move on for the next year, what would you have to face or build where you are?
What might you actually want, underneath the wanting-to-leave? A relationship, a rest, a self you have not let yourself be?
What is one thing you have been outsourcing to ‘the next place’ that you could give yourself right where you are? exercise
If leaving were not the answer, what would you do instead this month? one small thing

Moving on Purpose

This last page is not about a verdict, stay or go. It is about how you move: from choice, or from compulsion. You get to keep this life, or change it, but either way, let it be something you are choosing with open eyes, not something happening to you.

A note for you

You are allowed to keep going, and you are allowed to slow down. Neither is failure. The only thing that matters is that the choice is yours, made from what you want, not only from what you are avoiding.

Knowing what you know now, do you want to keep moving, slow down, or something in between? There is no wrong answer.
As you look honestly at your motion, what does the most grounded part of you want to do with it?
Then keep going, from choice. Let’s make sure it stays toward, not away.
How will you keep the ‘toward’ in the driver’s seat as you keep moving?
What early sign would tell you flight has taken over again, and what will you do when it does? one small thing
Then something in you is ready to move differently. That is not the end of the freedom; it is a truer version of it.
What do you want to face or build that the motion has been postponing?
What is one honest step toward that you could take, even a small one? one small thing

You do not have to stop moving to be free. You only have to know why you move, and choose it, every time.
