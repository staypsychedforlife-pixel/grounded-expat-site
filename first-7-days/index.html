Your First 7 Days Abroad

One small page a day, for the week you land.
Created by Stephanie Johnson, LICSW · The Grounded Expat

You just arrived. Before any of the big questions, this is a gentle companion for your first week — one small page a day. It won’t fix everything; it’ll just help you land.

Your writing saves automatically on this device and stays completely private to you. Use Print / Save as PDF to keep it, or Download my writing to export it.

This is an educational, self-reflection resource — not therapy, diagnosis, or a crisis service. If you’re struggling beyond what journaling can hold, please reach out to someone you trust, a licensed professional, or local emergency services. You deserve real support.
Day One

You’re Here

You don’t have to feel at home yet. Today, the only task is to arrive.

Where are you, right now, as you write this? Describe the actual room — the light, the sounds, the temperature. Let yourself be exactly here.

Day Two

Your Body First

Your nervous system reads ‘new’ as ‘unsafe’ and works overtime to keep up. Settle the body before the big questions.

What is your body asking you for today — rest, food, air, movement, stillness? Name it, and give it one.
Day Three

One Anchor

Abroad, routine is medicine. One familiar thing, repeated, is how your system learns it’s safe here.

Step Outside

Day Six

Name the Hard Part

Naming a hard thing shrinks it. Whatever you’re finding hard is normal — and allowed.

What’s been hardest so far? Just name it — no fixing required.
