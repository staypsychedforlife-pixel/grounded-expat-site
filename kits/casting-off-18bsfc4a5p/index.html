Casting Off

A companion for the goodbye.

Hi, and welcome. You are in one of the strangest stretches of this whole thing. You have decided to go, and now you are living in the space between. Already halfway gone in your mind, still fully here in your body, saying goodbye to a life while you are somehow still inside it.

Almost nobody prepares you for this part. The anticipatory grief, the weight of the last times, the guilt about the people you are leaving, the whiplash of being thrilled and heartbroken in the same afternoon. I am a therapist, and a nomad, and I have felt every bit of it. This kit is here to help you leave well, which does not mean leaving without grief. It means feeling the whole of it, saying your goodbyes with your whole heart, and walking toward the next thing anyway.

Take your time with the goodbyes. They matter. — Stephanie, LICSW

02How to Say Goodbye

Nobody teaches us how to do this. Here is how to leave a place and its people well.

Say the real goodbyes on purpose

The goodbyes that haunt us are the ones we did not actually have. Give the people and places that matter a chosen goodbye, not a rushed one.

Let the last times be last times

Do not numb your way past the last coffee, the last drive. Let yourself feel the weight. That weight is love, and it is worth feeling all the way through.

Have the conversations you have been avoiding

Tell people what they have meant to you. You will never regret having told someone they mattered. It is the unsaid ones that follow us.

Make a small ritual

Photograph the ordinary, keep a small object, walk your neighborhood one last time and thank it. Rituals help the heart catch up to what the mind already knows.

Be honest about staying in touch

You will not keep in touch with everyone the way you promise, and that is not a failure of love. Be intentional about the few you truly want to carry across the ocean.

Leave the door open, not the wound

The goal is not to leave without pain. The goal is to leave without regret. Warm, honest, complete.

03The Guilt of Going

The feeling that catches almost everyone off guard: the quiet ache about the people you are leaving behind.

Guilt is not proof you are doing something wrong

Their sense of loss is real. It is still not evidence that you are doing something wrong. Wanting a fuller life is not a harm.

Love is not a fixed amount that runs out with distance

You are not subtracting yourself from their lives. You are changing the shape of your presence in them, and presence has more shapes than proximity.

You are allowed to disappoint people you love

You cannot both go and prevent all of their sadness. A good, kind, devoted person is still allowed to choose themselves sometimes.

Turn toward the people, not into silence

Say "I am going, and it is hard for me too, and you matter to me." Guilt festers in avoidance and softens in honest contact.

The people who truly love you want you to have a life that is yours, even when your leaving costs them something. You can honor their loss and still get on the plane.
