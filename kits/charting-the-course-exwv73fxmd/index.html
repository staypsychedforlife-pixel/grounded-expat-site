Charting the Course

You keep imagining a life abroad. Here is where to actually begin.

Hi, and welcome. If you are here, you have probably been quietly imagining this for a while. A different life, somewhere else, a version of you that got up and went. And every time you get close to it, the whole thing balloons into something too big to hold, so you set it back down and call it someday.

I made this kit to make it smaller and more real. I am a therapist, and a nomad, and I promise you the people already living the life you are picturing did not have it figured out either. They just took a first step while they were still unsure. This kit is here to help you take yours.

We are going to move through four gentle stages. You do not have to rush them.

Let's begin. — Stephanie, LICSW

01The First-Steps Roadmap

The whole thing feels enormous because you are looking at all of it at once. A life abroad is not one giant decision, it is a stack of small ones, taken in order. Here is the order, and the single first move for each.

1

Clarity — what are you actually reaching for?

Not the Instagram version. The real one. Do you want to slow down or speed up? Escape something, or move toward something? The clearer you are here, the easier every other decision becomes.

First move: Spend twenty minutes with the Running Toward or Running Away journal below.
2

Which shape — a base, or a backpack?

Settle in one place, keep moving, or slow-travel a few places a year. Very different lives. Pick a direction to start, not forever.

First move: Read the Which Life Fits You guide below and notice which one your body leans toward.
3

Money — the number that unlocks the door

You probably need less than you fear. Mostly you need to know your number: what a month actually costs where you are going.

First move: Pick one place you are drawn to and look up the monthly cost of living for one person. One number, written down.
4

Where — narrow the whole world to a shortlist

The world is too big to choose from at once. Let it come down to two or three places that fit your clarity and budget. Not forever. Just where to start.

First move: Write down three places that keep pulling at you. First instinct.
5

The legal path — the part that feels scary and usually is not

Every country has a route in: a tourist stay, a nomad visa, a work permit. You do not need to be an expert. You need to know which one door is yours.

First move: Search "[your top country] digital nomad visa" and read one official-looking page. That is enough for today.
6

Income — how the life pays for itself

A remote job, freelancing, a business, savings for a season, or a blend. This one often takes longest, which is why you start it early and gently, not perfectly.

First move: Write one sentence: "While I am abroad, money will come from ______." A rough answer counts.
7

Timing — turn "someday" into a season

"Someday" is where dreams go to wait forever. Attaching your move to an actual season, even a loose one, changes everything.

First move: Finish this out loud: "If I did this, I would want to go around ______."
You do not have to do all seven. Pick the single one that feels the most alive, or the most stuck, and take just its first move. That is how every person now living the life you are imagining actually began. Not with the whole staircase. With one stair. You are closer than it feels.

02Which Life Fits You

"Living abroad" is at least three different lives, and they feel completely different from the inside. Notice which one your body relaxes into, not which one sounds most impressive.

The Expat — a life with a base

How it feels: like planting. Slow, then suddenly, home.

You pick one place and actually live there. A regular cafe, a doctor, a rhythm. You unpack all the way.

Fits you if: you crave roots more than variety, and want to belong somewhere rather than visit everywhere.

The Nomad — a life with a backpack

How it feels: like flying. Exhilarating, a little untethered, endlessly new.

You keep moving, following weather or visas or curiosity, trading a fixed home for a wide-open map.

Fits you if: novelty feeds you and routine drains you, and no lease sounds like freedom, not fear.

The Slow Traveler — a life in seasons

How it feels: like sampling. Unhurried, curious, options open.

Two or three places a year, long enough to feel each one, not long enough to fully uproot. The gentlest way in.

Fits you if: you want more than a vacation but are not ready to fully commit, and would like to test the water first.

You are not signing a contract. Almost everyone changes shape over time. Choose the one to start with, the one your body already leans toward. You can always change your mind once you are living instead of imagining.
